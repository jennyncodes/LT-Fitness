Nutrition for Kids

LT Fitness provides research-backed information and resources on kids’ nutrition. We understand how important nutrition is when it comes to the growth and development of your child. A balanced diet is essential for the well-being of your child. We try to make learning about nutrition fun with our interactive classes. We can also help you connect with a certified nutritionist to get a tailored nutrition plan for your child.

Some of our nutrition services

  • Nutrition Information – Research-backed information and resources on general nutritional best practices.
  • Culinary Workshops – A fun way to learn about nutrition while learning to cook easy and nutritious meals.
  • Customized Nutrition Plans – Connect with a certified nutritionist to get a tailor-made plan to meet the unique nutrition needs of your child.

Bread, Cereals and Potatoes - Provide a Source of Energy

Recommended servings per day: 4-6+ servings

1 Serving can be any of the following

  • 1 slice of bread or a small bread roll
  • 1 small bowl of cereal
  • 1 medium potato
  • 3 scoops of boiled rice or pasta

More active kids would need more servings to have enough energy.

Fruit and Vegetables - Provide Vitamins and Minerals

Recommended servings per day: 5 servings

1 Serving can be any of the following

  • 1 medium sized fresh fruit
  • Small glass of unsweetened pure fruit juice
  • Bowl of home-made vegetable soup
  • 3 scoops of vegetable salad

Kids with vitamin deficiency would need more servings.

Milk, Cheese and Yoghurt - Provide Calcium

Recommended servings per day: 3 servings

1 Serving can be any of the following

  • 1 glass of full fat milk
  • 1 small bowl of yogurt
  • 2 cheese slices
  • A small piece of hard cheese

Meat, Fish and Alternatives - Provide Protein

Provide a source of energy

Recommended servings per day: 2-3 servings

1 Serving can be any of the following

  • Small pork or lamb chop
  • Medium fillet of fish
  • 2 eggs
  • 6 tablespoons of baked beans, peas, lentils

Ocassional Healthy Snacks

Recommended to give occasionally and not on a daily basis

Some healthy snack options

  • Crackers or rice cakes
  • Small sandwiches
  • Homemade milkshake using yoghurt and fruit
  • Vegetable slices, sticks or wedges

Avoid sugary snacks and sodas.
